    ▥ Subfloor

    Lay ¾" (23/32") T&G rated subfloor (plywood or OSB, Exposure 1) over the joists.

    Panel4×8 ¾" T&G
    Strength axis⟂ to joists (8' runs N–S)
    Pieces

    Placement strategy

    1 · Run the 8' length across the joists; the 4' end joints land on a joist center (8' = six 16" bays). ✓

    2 · Stagger end joints by 4' course-to-course (brick pattern) — never let four corners meet.

    3 · T&G long edges support themselves between joists — no blocking needed. (Square-edge panels → add 2× blocking under the long seams.)

    4 · Leave a ⅛" gap at all panel ends/edges for expansion.

    5 · Glue + screw: bead of subfloor adhesive on every joist top, then #8 × 2" screws (or ring-shank nails) ~6" OC at edges, 12" OC in field. Glue kills squeaks.

    6 · Start in a corner with a full sheet; begin alternate courses with a half sheet to set the stagger; field-cut the perimeter.

    Numbered panels on the plan = the cut sequence. Buy ~8 sheets (≈6.3 of coverage + cutting waste).

    ▤ Wall Framing

    Recommended for the above-grade walls: 2×6 @ 16" OC, SPF or SYP #2.

    Studs2×6 @ 16" OC
    Platesdouble top + single bottom
    Corners3-stud posts (×4)
    Stud count

    Why 2×6 over 2×4 here:

    • Lands flush on the 5½" sill — load drops straight down stud → plate → rim → sill → CMU.

    • Deeper cavity ≈ R-21 vs R-13/15 — matters for a conditioned room in tornado country.

    • Room for bath + kitchen drain/vent runs in the walls.

    • Better for the tall (east) high-side wall of the shed roof and for wind.

    Notes

    • Interior non-bearing partitions can be 2×4.

    • Bottom plate need not be PT (the PT is the sill on the CMU).

    • Final size depends on high-wall height + local energy/wind code — confirm.

    To switch the drawing to 2×4: set studD: 3.5 in the CONFIG (CFG.wall).

    ⚓ Anchor Bolts

    WallCount

    ½" J-bolts, 7" min embed into grouted CMU cores. IRC R403.1.6: ≤6' OC, within 12" of each plate end, ≥2 per piece.

    • Tornado upgrade: tighten to 4' OC + 3"×3" plate washers (see note below).

    • Grout the core solid at each bolt.

    Build notes

    • Isolation joint: ½" expansion material between slab and foundation — pour the patio independent of the wall so they move separately.

    • Reinforce: #3 rebar @ 18" OC each way (or 6×6 W1.4 WWM), kept up on chairs at mid-slab.

    • Drainage: slope the finished surface ¼" per foot away from the foundation (2½" fall over 10').

    • Edges: thicken the free edges (turn-down) to ~8"–12"; set below frost depth if it carries any load.

    • Concrete: ≈100 sf × 4" ≈ 1.25 cu yd; add ~10% waste → order ~1.4 cu yd (4000 psi, air-entrained).

    • Confirm the south door swing/threshold height works with the finished slab elevation.

    • Verify local frost depth & setbacks before pouring.

    ⚠ Tornado Shelter Scope

    This sheet shows ordinary IRC platform framing. A rated tornado shelter is governed by ICC 500 / FEMA P-361 — wind-borne debris, uplift, and connection loads that exceed IRC and require an engineer's stamp. Treat this as a framing layout, not a certified shelter design.

    ⚠ Field Verify

    • Confirm wall lengths are EXTERIOR (out-to-out) — the whole model depends on it.

    • Out-of-square: N−S = ¼", E−W = 2". Within shim tolerance but verify diagonals.

    • All 4 corner heights + mid-span wobble (only NE ~40⅝" known).

    • Windows/door do NOT reach the top of the wall (sill runs continuously over them).

    • Door · E swing direction; confirm 31¾" opening.

    • Door · S (south wall): confirm 36" R.O. and swing direction. Shown 12" from west corner — verify no conflict with interior stair or egress path.

    • Door · W (west wall): confirm 36" R.O. and swing direction. Left (N) edge on the wall centerline (84⅜" from S corner) — verify the opening clears the low (W) shed-roof plate height and any interior layout.

    • Wood stove — clearance-to-combustibles NOT shown (footprint only). Confirm rear/side clearances from the stove's UL listing; add a listed heat shield if you need reduced clearances to the E-wall framing.

    • Wood stove is on the E wall, a few feet north of the E door (backed square to the wall; hearth clears the door opening by ~1'-7"). Confirm the E-door swing/egress path stays clear of the hearth.

    • Chimney 3-2-10 rule: the straight-up flue exits near the high (E) ridge, so it needs only ~3 ft above the roof. Verify the exact penetration vs. the ridge beam & rafters (2" clearance, framed roof opening + flashing/storm collar).

    • Hearth pad must be listed non-combustible with the stove's required R-value/thickness. Class-A floor & roof penetrations need 2" clearance to combustibles (framed opening + radiation shield). Provide outside combustion air + a CO alarm for a basement stove.
