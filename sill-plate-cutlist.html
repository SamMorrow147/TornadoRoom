Corner Strategy

North and South plates are dominant — they run the full outer width of the CMU wall, corner to corner. East and West plates butt in between them.

1 Install North plate first — full width of the N wall top, corner to corner. This equals your measured EXTERIOR length: 121¼".
2 Install South plate same way. 121".
3 Drop West plates in between N and S plates — they butt against the inside faces (so W length = 240¾" − 5½" − 5½" = 229¾", split into two boards).
4 Drop East plates the same way (E length = 238¾" − 11" = 227¾", two boards), continuous over the door opening. The door is in the CMU wall face; the top of the wall is solid above it.

⚠ Your wall lengths are EXTERIOR (out-to-out) = the corner-to-corner length across the top of the CMU. The N/S plates ARE that length. Do NOT use the interior room dimension (~15" smaller each way) for plate length.

Shimming rule:

Under ¼" variation → sill seal foam absorbs it, no shim needed.

¼"–¾" → PT wood shims at each bolt + mid-span.

Over ¾" → grout parge + shims, or grind down high spots.

⚠ The highest point on the wall becomes your level reference. All low spots get shimmed UP to match it.

Installation Sequence

1
Level survey — measure all 4 corners and 4 mid-spans. Record on the measurements sheet. Identify high point.
2
Lay sill seal — roll foam gasket along full top of wall, including over window openings. Cut at door gap. Tape joints.
3
Set anchor bolt positions — per the blueprint (18 J-bolts). Grouting CMU cores? Do it now before plates go down.
4
Dry-fit North plate (N-1) — mark bolt hole locations, drill oversize holes (⅝"), check fit. Verify it's level with shims as needed.
5
Dry-fit South plate (S-1) — same process.
6
Fit West plates (W-1, W-2) — butt against inside face of N and S plates. Mark and drill bolt holes. Stagger butt joint over CMU core.
7
Fit East plates (E-S, E-N) — butt against inside face of N and S plates, gap at door opening.
8
Final install — remove plates, add shims where needed, re-lay sill seal, drop plates, install washers and nuts. Snug only — don't fully torque until all plates are down and leveled.
9
Torque and caulk — final torque bolts (hand tight + ½ turn). Caulk around bolt penetrations and any sill seal gaps.

Field Flags — Confirm Before Cutting

⚠ S wall is ¼" narrower than N wall — check if this affects N-1 vs S-1 length
